# Engagement Scope & Phasing — hold this line

**Written 2026-07-12.** Carrie has, over a few days, floated: owned website ·
local SEO / AI-search visibility · lead capture · AI appointment/response bot ·
social-media automation · luxury/builder positioning · custom-build lead gen.
That is an agency engagement, not a weekend site. The trade ("free because I'm
bored" ↔ a deal on Brook's next house) is real value and must map to a
**bounded** phase — not silently expand to cover all of it.

## The phases

### Phase 1 — the favor (covered by the house-deal trade)
Owned site build, tracking (GA4/GSC), local SEO + entity/NAP cleanup, GBP
optimization, the content/guides already built, lead-capture forms, launch.
*This is what the trade pays for. It's substantial and it's basically done
pending Carrie's access + the July 24 kickoff. Deliver it, ship it, done.*

### Phase 2 — business relationship (needs its own terms)
CRM integration + the **AI lead-response agent** (note: the bot being built now
is Phase 2, not Phase 1) + ongoing lead-routing/automation. **This has real
recurring cost:** every AI conversation is a metered API charge on Brook's
account, plus maintenance. Things with a per-unit cost are the easiest to not
give away — that's the natural, non-awkward place to set an arrangement
(monthly, or cost-plus, or a per-lead/success model).

### Phase 3 — ongoing agency work (needs its own terms)
Social-media automation, luxury/builder campaign, custom-build lead gen,
content operations. Recurring labor. Paid, or a defined revenue/referral
arrangement, or explicitly declined.

## How to hold it (warm, not lawyerly)
- The AI agent is the perfect Phase-1→2 hinge: build it as a **"look what I
  made you"** showcase (great pitch, fits the favor), but be explicit that
  *running it live monthly* is where a simple arrangement kicks in — because it
  literally costs money per conversation. True, concrete, not awkward.
- Frame Phases 2–3 as "the fun part where this becomes a real thing" — not a
  bill, an upgrade. She has budget (she pays $3k/mo for reels).
- One sentence in writing beats a handshake: "Phase 1 (the site) is the trade;
  the automation + ongoing stuff we'd set up properly as we go." Said once,
  early, kills six months of ambiguity.

## The honest friend note
"I'll do it free because I'm bored" + "an attractive Realtor said you're
brilliant" is a well-documented path to an unpaid six-month engagement. The
work is genuinely good and Carrie is genuinely a great flagship client — which
is exactly why it should become Brook's **first paid product**, not his most
elaborate favor. Keep the generosity on Phase 1; make Phases 2–3 the business.
