# Marketing firm data request — via Carrie

Goal: baseline what the ~$3k/mo firm actually delivers (see
`reports/10_facebook_reels_and_marketing_spend.md`). Carrie requests her own
data — normal client ask, framed as baselining, not auditing.

## Brook → Carrie (text)

> One more thing — can you ask your marketing company for a copy of your
> reports/results? I'm baselining everything before I change anything, and
> their numbers are part of the picture. Here's a message you can copy-paste
> to them 👇

## Carrie → firm (copy-paste)

> Hi! I'm doing a review of all my marketing with an analytics consultant
> (he does this for a living — being thorough, not unhappy!). Can you send
> over:
> 1. My monthly reports for the last 6 months, if you have them
> 2. How my monthly budget splits between content creation vs. ad
>    spend/boosting
> 3. From Ads Manager: what campaigns ran, what they were optimized for
>    (views? leads?), who they targeted, and what they cost per result
> 4. How many leads I've gotten from the page and how you count a "lead"
> 5. What's included in my package, just so I have it in writing
>
> Easiest might be adding him as a read-only analyst on the ad account /
> Business Suite — his email is brookd87@gmail.com. Whatever's simplest for
> you!

## Reading the response

1. **Real reports incl. lead counts + spend split** → baseline established;
   firm may be delivering fine. Compare cost-per-lead vs what owned channels
   produce over the next 90 days.
2. **Reach/views screenshots only, no leads/spend split** → consistent with
   the bimodal-boosting read in reports/10 — budget buying the cheapest
   metric. Pair with Carrie's own answer to "where do your leads come from."
3. **Stalling/gatekeeping a client's own ad account** → itself the answer;
   let Carrie observe it without editorializing.

Key field: campaign **objective** in item 3. Video-view campaigns vs
lead-generation campaigns settles the value question in one line.
