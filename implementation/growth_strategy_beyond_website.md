# Carrie — Growth Strategy Beyond the Website

**Working strategy, 2026-07-12.** Everything Carrie has said she wants, outside
the core site, organized into coherent plays and prioritized against her *real*
constraint. This is the Phase-2/3 material (see `engagement_scope_and_phasing.md`).

## What she's actually asked for (catalogued)
Across conversations: (1) "leads like Zillow without paying referral fees",
(2) luxury / custom-build growth via the Acadiana Home Builders partnership,
(3) "use AI more — so much value I'm not utilizing", (4) AI to run social media,
(5) a real-estate assistant bot, (6) an ebook, (7) "some type of coaching."

## The clarifying lens (this reframes everything)
These wants serve **different objectives** and cost **different amounts of her
time** — and her binding constraint is not money, it's **hours**. She's an
effectively-solo agent, single mom, already working flat out, already paying
~$3k/mo for social. So the filter isn't "is this a good AI idea" — it's:

> **Does this give her time back or convert leads she already has — or does it
> demand more of the one thing she can't spare?**

AI's real value *for her specifically* is **time leverage + conversion**, not
new business lines she'd have to staff. That single lens sorts her whole list.

## The plays, sorted

### PLAY 1 — Convert what she already generates *(highest ROI, lowest new effort)*
The site + lead magnet + **AI lead-response agent** (built) + review
distribution. She already creates demand (185 Google reviews, active social,
real production); it leaks at capture/response. Speed-to-lead + 24/7 AI response
+ owned-site capture = "leads like Zillow without the referral fee." **This is
the whole thesis and it mostly exists.** Fastest money, least new work from her.

### PLAY 2 — The luxury / new-construction vertical *(distinct GTM, real moat)*
The AHB partnership is a **channel**, not just a page — the builder publicly
names her as their realtor. This is a *separate motion* from her bread-and-butter:
spec-home marketing, custom-build buyer capture, luxury positioning. It's
defensible (nobody can copy "I represent AHB") and it's her stated growth market
— but it is **additive to**, not a replacement for, her core first-time/move-up
business. Needs: partnership scope + co-marketing budget (builders often have
one — money she may not be tapping).

### PLAY 3 — The content/social engine *(where the $3k/mo lives — measure before adding)*
She pays ~$3k/mo (~$36k/yr) to an outside firm, ROI unclear (our reels analysis:
real views, boost-shaped, no visible lead attribution). **This is the biggest
reallocation lever in her whole business.** The AI play here is *time leverage*:
turn one listing/reel into multi-platform posts, captions, a blog post, a
YouTube upload — AI-**assisted**, human voice intact (autopilot social is slop).
**Decision point, not a build:** get the firm's lead numbers first (checklist
already drafted). If it's producing → keep + augment. If it's buying views →
that $36k/yr funds everything else on this list.

### PLAY 4 — Info products / authority / coaching *(real, but define the audience first)*
The ebook is the on-ramp (built — as a lead magnet). "Coaching" is undefined and
the fork matters: **consumers** (buyer/seller guides, first-time-buyer
mini-course → lead gen, low effort, on-brand) vs. **agents** (teaching other
realtors AI/marketing → a *different business*, more of her time, competes with
selling houses). Consumer-facing = extends Play 1. Agent-facing = a genuine
pivot she should choose deliberately, not drift into.

## Priority (impact × fits-her-time)
1. **Play 1** — mostly built; finish + launch. Money now, minimal new effort.
2. **Play 3 decision** — measure the firm; potentially free up $36k/yr. Pure
   analysis, no build. Highest financial leverage per hour spent.
3. **Play 2** — real growth, needs her partnership details + is additive.
4. **Play 4 (consumer)** — cheap authority/lead content off the ebook engine.
5. **Play 4 (agent coaching)** — park it; revisit only if she *wants* a second
   business, not as a default.

## Do NOT (the shiny stuff)
- AI-autopilot social (voice is the asset).
- A coaching business she has to run before her lead machine even works.
- Buying more tools/tactics before Play 3's measurement says where money goes.
- Anything that adds hours before the automation that *removes* hours is live.

## The AI-curiosity thread
Her "I want to use AI more" is appetite, not a project — channel it into Plays
1–3 where it compounds, and let the assistant bot + content repurposing be the
tangible "wow, I'm using AI now" wins. Give her the feeling *and* the ROI.

## For the 24th — questions that lock direction
1. What does winning look like in 12 months — more closings, higher price point,
   or fewer hours for the same income? (Different plays serve each.)
2. The $3k/mo firm: what leads/closings can they show for it? (Gets Play 3 moving.)
3. AHB partnership: scope, exclusivity, naming/photo rights, co-marketing budget?
4. "Coaching" — for buyers/sellers, or for other agents? (Forks Play 4.)
5. Where does your time actually go now, and what would you *stop* doing if a
   system did it for you? (Points AI at the real bottleneck.)
