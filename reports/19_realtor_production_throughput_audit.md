## Executive Finding

An exact leaderboard for **ROAM-recorded closed residential transactions** in each target market is possible, but it cannot be produced honestly from public portals alone. The decisive dataset is an authorized ROAM MLS Closed export filtered by **property location and close date**. Off-MLS, non-ROAM, and otherwise unreported closings remain outside that leaderboard and must be disclosed or reported separately. Public evidence currently supports three different views:

1. **Verified annual production for locally based people:** RealTrends verifies submitted calendar-2025 residential sides and volume, but assigns location from the agent's physical location, not from the properties sold. It is a strong candidate list, not a Youngsville- or Lafayette-specific leaderboard.
2. **Actual market production by brokerage:** a public year-end report derived from ROAM MLS provides 2025 residential sold sides and volume for Lafayette Parish, Vermilion Parish, and Acadiana. This is property-geographic, but it stops at brokerage level.
3. **Directional production by person and town:** Zillow, Homes.com, and FastExpert expose useful transaction signals, but their periods, attribution rules, team treatment, geography, and coverage differ. These identify whom the MLS audit must test; they do not settle rank.

The strongest current conclusions are:

- **Sean Hettich is the clearest conventional individual throughput candidate in the public evidence.** He has 68 RealTrends-verified residential sides and $22.18 million for calendar 2025, plus the strongest repeated conventional-agent signal in Youngsville, Broussard, and Maurice portal histories.
- **Arla Slaughter leads the captured verified local-individual sample by 2025 volume:** $23.68 million on 56.2 sides. Sean leads that captured sample by sides. This is not a complete-market claim because RealTrends is submission- and threshold-based.
- **Stephen Hundley is a close production benchmark:** 66 verified sides and $18.89 million in 2025, with repeated public evidence across Youngsville, Broussard, Scott, and Lafayette.
- **Teams must be a separate leaderboard.** The TK Group reports 162 verified 2025 sides and $31.97 million. NAR's Destinee Allanson profile separately reports 44 individual sides/$6.9 million and 203 team sides/$33 million. Combining either team total with an individual's total would create a false ranking.
- **Price point changes the winner.** Charlie Baudoin generated $16.03 million on only 24 sides, approximately $668,000 per represented side. A volume leaderboard, closing-count leaderboard, and client-count leaderboard answer different questions.
- **Carrie's public current band is meaningful but not audited:** Homes.com displayed 34 one-year deals/$9.48 million while Zillow displayed 22 sales in the last 12 months. The gap itself shows why portal totals are not a production ledger.
- **Brokerage context is strong for eXp but does not transfer automatically to Carrie.** In the public ROAM-derived report, eXp had 1,003.5 listing-or-selling sides/$259.82 million in Lafayette Parish and 154 sides/$32.32 million in Vermilion Parish during 2025. Those are brokerage totals, not Carrie's.

## Why Public Portals Cannot Produce the Final Rank

### Period mismatch

Calendar 2025, trailing 12 months, lifetime history, three-year market history, and an undisclosed period cannot be sorted together. The evidence CSV stores these in separate columns.

### Team attribution

Zillow can label sales to a team leader; team and individual profiles can overlap. Homes.com can expose large builder/broker/team histories under a person. This is especially important for Keaty, Robbie Breaux, David Wenger, Lana Soileau, and Milton builder representatives.

### Geography mismatch

RealTrends ranks by physical location. Zillow's town count has no window. Homes' Milton area extends beyond Milton. ZIP codes extend beyond municipal limits. Broussard crosses Lafayette and St. Martin parishes. Use declared, versioned boundary polygons first, then publish separate ZIP/parish views.

### Property-class mismatch

Builder portfolios, land, mobile/manufactured homes, leases, commercial, referral business, and off-market closings can enter or leave a source depending on its rules. The primary comparison should be closed residential 1-4 unit property; publish land/lots and other classes separately.

### Coverage mismatch

RealTrends requires participation and thresholds. FastExpert is opt-in. Portal feeds can be incomplete or manually supplemented. No public absence proves no production.

## Definitive ROAM Audit Specification

### Authorization first

Do not share an MLS login or export protected rows informally. Ask Carrie's broker/ROAM Participant to generate the report, or request an authorized aggregate/custom report through the [REALTOR Association of Acadiana ROAM MLS](https://realtoracadiana.com/roam-mls/) and its [staff directory](https://realtoracadiana.com/staff-directory/). Obtain a written statement covering internal analysis, retention, third-party processing, and whether **named competitor aggregates or rankings may be published**. Default to an internal leaderboard; publish named rankings only with explicit ROAM authorization and sponsoring-broker approval.

The current [ROAM Rules v2.2.2, revised May 11, 2026](https://realtoracadiana.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/ROAM-MLS-Rules-Regulations-v.2.2.2.final_.pdf) matter:

- The sold price is the actual consideration at closing; public-record price must not replace it.
- Data exports to unauthorized third parties are restricted, and login credentials cannot be shared.
- Public claims based on MLS information must state the period and use substantially: `Based on information from the ROAM MLS for the period [date] through [date].`
- Public sold/comparable use is restricted to aggregated statistical analysis.
- Brokerage Back Office agent productivity/ranking reports are limited to the Participant and affiliated subscribers unless ROAM authorizes the use.

### Required periods

1. Calendar 2025: `2025-01-01` through `2025-12-31`.
2. Rolling 12 complete months: through the last complete month at extraction.
3. 2026 YTD: through the same month/day cutoff used for the 2025 comparison.

### Required fields

```text
originating_mls, listing_key, mls_number, parcel_id,
street_address, latitude, longitude, city, postal_code, parish, subdivision,
property_type, property_subtype, new_construction_flag,
status, list_date, close_date, close_price,
original_list_price, last_list_price, cumulative_dom,
list_agent_mls_id, co_list_agent_ids,
buyer_agent_mls_id, co_buyer_agent_ids,
list_office_id, buyer_office_id,
team_id_if_available, modification_timestamp
```

Exclude client names, remarks, photos, compensation fields, documents, and contact information. Keep a separate identity table with LREC license ID, legal name, aliases, MLS member IDs, brokerage effective dates, team effective dates, and entity type. Use the [Louisiana Real Estate Commission](https://lrec.gov/) only to validate identity/status; LREC does not supply production.

### Geography

- Assign each closing by point-in-polygon against a versioned, named boundary dataset.
- Retain postal city, ZIP, and parish as separate secondary dimensions.
- Use the [Lafayette Consolidated Government property-address/GIS resources](https://www.lafayettela.gov/business-development/planning-and-development/planning-zoning-and-development-applications/property-addresses/) and an official [Broussard boundary reference](https://www.cityofbroussard.com/home/showpublisheddocument/766/638010145521600000).
- Milton is not an incorporated municipality. Use the current [U.S. Census Bureau Milton CDP, GEOID 2250815](https://tigerweb.geo.census.gov/tigerwebmain/Files/acs25/tigerweb_acs25_cdp_la.html) or a documented custom service-area polygon, and label the chosen definition explicitly.
- Store the source file, geographic vintage, retrieval date, and checksum for every boundary so reruns use the same geography.
- Treat `Lafayette city`, `Lafayette Parish`, and `Acadiana` as different markets.

### Attribution and formulas

For each transaction, listing-agent weights must sum to one listing side and buyer-agent weights must sum to one buyer side.

```text
listing_sides(agent) = sum(listing_weight)
buyer_sides(agent) = sum(buyer_weight)
total_sides(agent) = listing_sides + buyer_sides

listing_volume(agent) = sum(close_price * listing_weight)
buyer_volume(agent) = sum(close_price * buyer_weight)
represented_volume(agent) = listing_volume + buyer_volume

unique_transactions(agent) = count(distinct closing credited on either side)
unique_property_volume(agent) = sum(close_price once per distinct closing)
```

- Use ROAM-native production credit or an authorized broker production allocation when supplied. Do not solicit competitors' private compensation records. If no production allocation exists, split co-agent credit equally and flag `assumed_equal`.
- A person representing both sides receives two represented sides and twice the sale price in represented volume, but only one unique transaction and one unique-property sale price.
- Deduplicate cross-MLS/relisted rows by `parcel_id + close_date + close_price`; use normalized address when parcel ID is absent.
- Keep non-member/unrepresented sides in a separate bucket instead of reallocating them.
- Separate builder/new-construction, flat-fee/list-entry, resale, land/lots, leases, commercial, and 5+ unit multifamily.

### Required leaderboards

For each target market and period, publish separately:

1. Individual agents by unique transactions.
2. Individual agents by total sides.
3. Individual agents by represented volume.
4. Listing agents by listing sides and listing volume.
5. Buyer agents by buyer sides and buyer volume.
6. Teams by the same metrics.
7. Brokerages by the same metrics.

Also report median sale price, median cumulative DOM, median list-to-sale ratio, and cancellation/withdrawal context for listing-side work. Volume alone rewards price point; sides alone rewards throughput; unique transactions is the most intuitive closing count.

### Quality controls

- Resolve every MLS ID to an effective-dated LREC identity and brokerage/team.
- Reject or investigate missing IDs, zero prices, duplicate parcels, more than two whole represented sides per transaction, and overlapping team attribution.
