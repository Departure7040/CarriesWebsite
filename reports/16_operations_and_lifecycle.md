## (a) Why this report exists

Everything else in this project — the GBP fix, the new carriebilleaud.com,
the local pages, the review system — is **acquisition**: getting the right
people to find Carrie and raise a hand. This report covers **everything after
the hand goes up**, because a lead that rings a phone nobody answers is
Zillow's lead next week — literally. The stated goal is "leads like Zillow
without the referral fees," and Zillow's actual product isn't leads, it's
*response infrastructure*: a connection service that puts a live agent on the
phone in under a minute. If we generate our own leads but respond like a solo
agent mid-showing (call back in 3 hours), we've built a beautiful funnel that
empties into the ground. Acquisition and operations are one system; this
report maps the operations half.

Nothing here assumes new spending. Most of it is configuration of tools Carrie
already pays for through eXp, plus decisions about who does what — which are
[client-confirm] items for the July 24 kickoff.

## (b) The lead lifecycle map

Eight stages. For each: what a solo agent typically does, where leads die,
what top-producing teams do differently, and the right tool/workflow at
Carrie's scale (~50 families/year per her Instagram bio [client-confirm]).

### 1. Capture
- **Solo default:** Leads arrive scattered — site form to email inbox, Facebook
  DMs, Instagram "DM HOME" keyword, GBP calls, Calendly bookings, sign calls,
  texts from past clients. No single list of "everyone who inquired this week."
- **Where leads die:** In the inbox and the DMs. An unanswered Facebook message
  from Tuesday is invisible by Thursday. [best-practice]
- **Top teams:** Every source pipes into one CRM automatically. Nothing lives
  only in an inbox.
- **At Carrie's scale:** BoldTrail (eXp's included CRM) is built for exactly
  this — it accepts leads from forms, portals, and social. The work is wiring
  every source in, not buying anything. Her Calendly link on GBP [verified] is
  already a good capture asset; bookings should also land in the CRM.

### 2. First response (speed-to-lead)
- **Solo default:** Respond when free — after the showing, after the closing,
  that evening. Industry average response time is measured in hours.
- **Where leads die:** Right here, more than anywhere else. This is the
  single largest leak in the funnel (see section (c) for the evidence).
- **Top teams:** Contact within 5 minutes, every time, via a combination of
  instant auto-text ("Got your message — is now a bad time to call?") plus a
  human dialing within minutes. Zillow Flex/Premier's connection service is
  this, productized.
- **At Carrie's scale:** BoldTrail supports instant auto-text/auto-email on
  new lead + mobile push notification. Configure it once and the 5-minute
  window is covered even when she's in a listing appointment. Zero new cost.

### 3. Nurture (not-ready-yet leads)
- **Solo default:** One or two follow-ups, then the lead goes quiet and so
  does the agent. But most online leads transact 3–12+ months out.
  [best-practice]
- **Where leads die:** The 2nd-through-12th touch that never happens. The lead
  buys eight months later — with whichever agent stayed visible.
- **Top teams:** Automated long-term drip (market updates, listing alerts) +
  scheduled human touches, driven by CRM tasks, not memory.
- **At Carrie's scale:** BoldTrail's smart campaigns and behavioral alerts
  (lead re-visits the site, favorites a listing → agent gets pinged) are the
  right tool and already included. Her $3k/mo social content is actually a
  nurture asset here — if leads are in a database that sees it. CRM usage
  discipline is unknown [client-confirm]; this is the kickoff question that
  decides whether nurture exists at all.

### 4. Appointment
- **Solo default:** Phone/text tag to find a time.
- **Where leads die:** Three rounds of "what about Thursday?" — warm leads
  cool during scheduling friction.
- **Top teams:** Self-service booking with automated reminders; no-show rates
  drop when a confirmation text goes out day-before and hour-before.
- **At Carrie's scale:** She already has Calendly [verified]. Extend it: put
  the booking link in the auto-text from stage 2, on the new site's contact
  page, and in email signatures. Turn on SMS reminders.

### 5. Active client (searching/showing)
- **Solo default:** This is where solo agents are genuinely great — high-touch,
  personal, responsive. Carrie's 185 five-star Google reviews [verified] say
  she excels here.
- **Where leads die:** Rarely here directly — but this stage *eats the time*
  that stages 2 and 3 needed. Every hour showing houses is an hour of
  unanswered new leads. This is the structural solo-agent trap.
- **Top teams:** Showing partners absorb time-intensive buyer tours; the lead
  agent stays available for new business and negotiations.
- **At Carrie's scale:** No change yet — but this trap is exactly what the
  growth triggers in section (e) watch for, and it's why the Jake/Blake/Arrow
  question matters (section (c)).

### 6. Under contract
- **Solo default:** The agent personally chases inspections, appraisal,
  lender docs, title, deadlines — 15–20+ hours of coordination per
  transaction. [best-practice]
- **Where leads die:** Not the current deal — the *next* ones. Contract-to-close
  admin is the biggest consumer of hours that should go to new leads. Missed
  deadlines also create the only real E&O/reputation risk in the funnel.
- **Top teams:** A transaction coordinator (TC) runs the checklist; the agent
  handles negotiation and relationship only.
- **At Carrie's scale:** SkySlope (eXp's included transaction platform,
  [verified] — eXp agents use it for forms, signatures, and broker file
  compliance) is the system of record. The question is who drives it —
  Carrie, someone on the roster, or a contract TC (see section (e)).

### 7. Close
- **Solo default:** Closing gift, warm handshake, done.
- **Where leads die:** Nothing dies — but the single highest-leverage 5 minutes
  of the entire relationship gets skipped: the review ask at the closing
  table, when goodwill peaks.
- **Top teams:** Review request is a scripted, same-day step; the client is
  moved into a post-close plan before the file is archived.
- **At Carrie's scale:** Already covered by the review system in the 30-day
  plan (`07_30_60_90_day_plan.md`) — the ops job is making it a checklist item
  on every closing, not a when-remembered task.

### 8. Post-close reviews & referrals
- **Solo default:** Occasional social contact; the past client hears from
  their agent when the agent has a listing to promote.
- **Where leads die:** The referral that goes to another agent because "my
  realtor" faded from memory. Past clients + sphere are the cheapest lead
  source in real estate — and the one Zillow can't tax. [best-practice]
- **Top teams:** Systematic past-client program: home-anniversary touch,
  annual equity/market check-in, client events, consistent social presence.
- **At Carrie's scale:** BoldTrail drip + her existing strong social output
  cover most of this if past clients are actually *in the database* with
  close dates recorded. For her luxury/custom-build ambition, this stage is
  the engine: luxury and builder-partnership business runs on referrals and
  reputation far more than on search traffic. [best-practice]

**Summary of the leaks, ranked:** (1) first response speed, (2) long-term
nurture that never happens, (3) time cannibalized by contract-to-close admin,
(4) unsystematic referral cultivation. All four are fixable with tools she
already pays for plus role clarity.

## (c) The solo-with-a-roster problem

Zillow shows "Carrie Billeaud Team" with 4 members — Carrie, Jake, Blake,
Arrow [verified, `public_assets.yaml`]. Brook's working belief is that she
operates **effectively solo** [client-confirm]. Both can be true (portal
"teams" are often licensing/branding arrangements, past members, or family
with licenses), but the ops plan is completely different depending on the
answer, so it must be resolved at kickoff, not assumed:

1. **Who are Jake, Blake, and Arrow — really?** Active buyer's agents?
   Licensed but inactive? Which of them, if any, takes a lead handoff today?
2. **Who answers a brand-new lead, and how fast?** If Carrie is in a listing
   appointment when the site form fires at 2pm Tuesday, what actually happens?
3. **Who coordinates transactions?** Does anyone drive SkySlope besides
   Carrie? Is there any TC, even part-time/contract?
4. **What does the CRM actually contain?** Is BoldTrail (or its kvCORE
   predecessor) a working database or an untouched login?

**Why speed-to-lead is the hill to die on:** it is the most-replicated
finding in lead-conversion research. The MIT/InsideSales Lead Response
Management Study found the odds of *contacting* a web lead drop **100x**
between a 5-minute and a 30-minute response, and the odds of *qualifying*
that lead drop **21x** over the same window [source: MIT Lead Response
Management Study, Oldroyd et al.; corroborated by InsideSales, which reports
8x+ conversion lift for sub-5-minute response]. Online real estate leads are
comparison-shopping in the moment — the first credible responder usually
wins the conversation. This is the entire mechanical basis of Zillow's value
proposition; matching it in-house is how "leads like Zillow without referral
fees" stops being a slogan.

## (d) Tool stack: has / underuses / add / don't buy

### Already included via eXp [verified via 2026-07-11 searches]
- **BoldTrail** (the rebuilt kvCORE — eXp agents choose BoldTrail, Lofty, or
  Cloze inside the standard ~$85/mo eXp tech fee): AI-assisted CRM, branded
  IDX website, smart drip campaigns, behavioral alerts, lead routing,
  auto-text on new lead, mobile app.
- **SkySlope**: transaction management — forms, e-signature, document upload
  to eXp's broker/compliance team.
- **Calendly** on GBP [verified] — hers, not eXp's, but already in place.

### Likely underused [client-confirm at kickoff]
- BoldTrail auto-responders and drip campaigns (probably never configured).
- Behavioral listing alerts (lead activity pings).
- The database itself — past clients with close dates and home anniversaries.
- Source tracking: which of GBP / site / social / sign calls actually produce
  closings. Q14 in `09_questions_for_client.md` exists because nobody knows.

### How the new site's leads should route
Form submit → **BoldTrail CRM** (native form/API integration, or email-parse
as fallback) → **instant auto-text + auto-email to the lead** (with the
Calendly link) → **push/SMS alert to Carrie's phone** → if no human contact
logged in X minutes, escalation reminder. The site must plug into a system,
not an inbox. `info@carriebilleaud.com` as the *only* destination is the
failure mode we're designing against. The site build
(`production_site_plan.md`) should treat the BoldTrail webhook/parser address
as a launch requirement, same tier as DNS.

### Worth adding at her scale
- **Nothing paid, yet.** Configuration before acquisition of new tools.
- If (and only if) after 60 days the CRM shows leads still waiting >15 min
  during business hours: a contract ISA/call service or a shared answering
  arrangement — a per-lead cost decision made on data.

### Explicitly NOT worth buying yet
- A standalone CRM (Follow Up Boss, Sierra, etc.) — real money ($60–500+/mo)
  duplicating what eXp includes; revisit only if BoldTrail is genuinely
  fought-with after honest configuration effort.
- Paid portal leads (Zillow Premier etc.) — the exact spend this project
  exists to make optional.
- AI chatbot/ISA subscriptions — solves a volume problem she doesn't have yet.
- Dialers, mass-texting platforms — team-scale tools.

## (e) Growth triggers — when to add people

Tie hiring to observable thresholds, not vibes. All thresholds
[best-practice]; calibrate at kickoff once real volume is confirmed
[client-confirm].

| Trigger | Signal | Add |
|---|---|---|
| **Hire a TC** (first hire, contract per-file ~$300–500/transaction) | Sustained ~2–3+ closings/month, or any missed contract deadline, or admin visibly displacing lead follow-up | Contract transaction coordinator driving SkySlope |
| **Add a showing partner** | Regularly turning down or delaying showings; buyer tours consuming 3+ weekdays/week | Licensed showing assistant (per-showing or small split) |
| **Add an ISA / answering layer** | CRM data shows business-hours leads waiting >15 min more than ~25% of the time, or lead volume >40–50 new leads/month | Contract ISA or call-handling service |
| **Formalize the team** | All three above triggered and holding | The Jake/Blake/Arrow conversation becomes a real org-design decision |

The sequence matters: **TC first** (buys back the most hours per dollar),
showing support second, ISA third. Most solo agents buy in the reverse order
and regret it. [best-practice]
