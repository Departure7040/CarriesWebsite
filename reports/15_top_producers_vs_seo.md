# Flip the Lens: Who Actually Sells the Most Houses vs. Who Wins Search

**Date:** 2026-07-11
**Reads with:** `reports/11_top10_ranking_drivers.md` (the SEO-visible top 10) and `data/top10_realtors.csv`. This report inverts that lens: instead of asking *who ranks*, it asks *who produces* — then checks how much the two leaderboards overlap and what the overlap (and the gaps) mean for Carrie's strategy.

**Tagging key:** `[source-shown]` = a number a portal or ranking site displays; NOT independently verified, NOT harmonized across sources · `[self-reported]` = agent/broker-claimed · `[verified]` = confirmed against a live fetch in this project · `[inferred]` = our read of the pattern, not a sourced fact. **No number in section (b) or (c) should be read as "true production." MLS production data is not public; every figure here is what some third party chose to display.**

---

## (a) Framing: Two Different Leaderboards

SEO visibility and sales production are different scoreboards, and **Carrie's actual goal is the second one.** Report 11's top 10 is a list of who *shows up in search* — a means, not the end. If some agent outsold everyone in Lafayette 2:1 while having zero web presence, that agent's existence would prove SEO is optional for winning the market, and this entire project would need reframing.

So this report is a falsification check on the SEO plan: **do the people who rank also sell, and do the people who sell also rank?** Three possible answers, three different strategic implications:

1. **High overlap** → search visibility and production travel together; SEO is (at least correlated with) the winning channel. Full speed ahead.
2. **Low overlap, producers dominate elsewhere** → the biggest producers win on sphere, referral, and team operations, not search. SEO is then *not* the incumbents' moat — which makes it an under-defended lane for a challenger, but also means SEO alone won't produce a top producer.
3. **SEO-visible but production-light agents exist** → direct evidence that ranking ≠ closing; visibility without conversion/ops is a vanity metric.

The data below shows elements of all three — mostly (2), with instructive cases of (3).

### What the three quadrants say

**Both lists (SEO + production):** Fonseca, Breaux, and Keaty-the-brokerage anchor this quadrant, with a middle band (Broussard, Hettich, McCubbin, Hillard) at moderate scale. So SEO visibility and production *do* co-occur — for maybe 6–7 of the SEO top 10. But note the mechanism differences: Fonseca produces so much that portals rank *for* her; Keaty ranks as a brand, not as its individual producers `[inferred]`.

**Production without SEO — the big finding:** At least **nine named agents with production signals equal to or better than most of the SEO top 10 have zero presence in the SEO top 10** — including Matthew Delcambre, arguably the single most credible Lafayette production story in the mined data. Real Producers' entire Acadiana top 4 is invisible in search. These agents are running on some combination of sphere-of-influence, repeat/referral, brokerage lead-routing (Keaty's machine feeding Delcambre `[inferred]`), and team operations. **They are winning the game Carrie wants to win, without playing the game this project optimizes.** That's the humbling half of the flip.

**SEO without production — the cautionary tales:** Kris Bourque holds SEO position #10 with 22 lifetime sales and $708K in the last 12 months `[source-shown]`. Cassidy Stoma recurs across 7 market tags with fragmented, low-confidence production. **Ranking is demonstrably achievable without meaningful production behind it — which means ranking, by itself, converts nothing.** A SERP position is a shop window; these two cases show the window can be lit while the store is quiet.

**Overlap verdict: PARTIAL — roughly 60% of the SEO top 10 shows real production, but the market's heaviest hitters are mostly NOT competing in search at all.**

## (e) What This Changes: SEO Is the Asymmetric Lane — and Only Half the Job

Two conclusions, and they pull in the same direction:

**1. The incumbents aren't defending search — so search is Carrie's asymmetric lane.** If Delcambre, Senpeng, and the Real Producers top 4 were all pouring effort into SERP dominance, Carrie would be entering a war of attrition against better-resourced players. They aren't. The heaviest producers win on sphere, referral, and team ops, and their search presence is incidental (portal residue) or absent. The agents who *do* hold SERP positions include two with production an order of magnitude below Carrie's own displayed numbers — meaning the bar to enter the SEO leaderboard is set by Kris Bourque's Wix site, not by Matthew Delcambre's $41M. **A mid-tier producer with 185 Google reviews attacking under-defended SERPs is a legitimate asymmetry.** Nothing in this report weakens the plan in reports 11/04b — the Carencro/Scott/Maurice thin markets, the buyer-intent gap, the valuation tooling — it *strengthens* the case that those positions are takeable.

**2. But the production-without-SEO quadrant proves visibility doesn't close houses — ops and conversion are the mandatory second half.** The market's actual winners are winning on what happens *after* (or entirely without) the click: referral systems, speed-to-lead, brokerage lead-routing, repeat-client machinery. And the SEO-without-production cases (Bourque, Stoma) show what Carrie's future looks like if she ranks and nothing else changes: a lit shop window, a quiet store. Every SERP position this project wins hands Carrie a lead that her current stack — no valuation tool, no calculator, broken portal attribution, eXp-platform technical debt `[verified, production_site_plan.md]` — is not yet built to catch and convert.

**Bridge to report 16:** the next report must cover the ops/conversion half — lead capture, response-time discipline, portal attribution repair (the Realtor.com "1 closing" fix is priority one for displayed credibility), review-platform diversification (Zillow 29 vs. Google 185), and the referral/sphere systems the invisible top producers actually run on. SEO gets Carrie found; report 16 is about what happens in the sixty seconds after.
