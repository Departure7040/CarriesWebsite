## What's hurting [verified unless noted]

1. **Your Google Business Profile's name is keyword/area-decorated and needs
   manager access.** The name shows as "Carrie Billeaud Realtor | Acadiana &
   Surrounding Area | eXp Realty" — Google requires the real-world name used
   on your signage/stationery, and a decorated name like this risks
   suspension or a forced edit. It also may be why our own searches for the
   profile came back empty even though it's live and strong — worth fixing
   carefully (one change at a time, documented). The website link on the
   profile also has a Facebook tracking artifact instead of a clean link.
2. **Inconsistent identity.** 3 phone numbers, 5 addresses, 3 emails across
   your profiles. Google and AI tools can't tell which is really you, so they
   trust all of it less. (Your GBP address matches one existing variant —
   relevant to, but not deciding, which one is canonical.) It even extends to
   Facebook: you have two separate pages — the large one (5,550 likes) and a
   business page your TikTok/Instagram actively link to — and it's not clear
   which one is meant to be primary.
3. **Your website blocks AI crawlers.** A technical misconfiguration on the
   eXp-hosted site blocks ChatGPT, Perplexity, and similar tools from reading
   it. Fixable via an eXp support ticket.
4. **Your agent page has no bio.** The page meant to tell Google who you are
   goes straight from the header to a contact form.
5. **Review concentration on Google/Zillow, gaps elsewhere.** 185 on Google,
   29 on Zillow, but 2 on Realtor.com and 0 on Homes.com. The gap now is about
   spreading your strong reputation to other platforms, not building Google
   from zero. [competitor data verified 2026-07-08]

## 5 things NOT to waste money on

1. **Backlink packages / "citation blasts"** — spam that risks more entity
   confusion, the exact problem we're fixing.
2. **"AI SEO" gimmicks** (llms.txt hacks, AI-written page farms) — AI
   visibility comes from the same fundamentals as Google visibility.
3. **A paid website rebuild before the basics** — the eXp site has limits, but
   bio, metadata, and content fixes come first and are free.
4. **Buying leads from portals as a *substitute* for this work** — rented
   visibility disappears the day you stop paying.
5. **Review-gating or incentivized-review tools** — against Google policy and
   can get reviews removed or the profile suspended.
