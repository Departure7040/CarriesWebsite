# Executive Summary — Carrie Billeaud SEO & Lead Generation Audit

*Prepared by Brook DuBose · July 2026 · Plain English, no jargon without translation*

## The one-paragraph version

Carrie, you have good raw materials — a 5.0-star Zillow profile with 29 reviews,
a strong Facebook following (5,500+ likes), real production, and a recognizable
name in Lafayette. But the public SEO/entity layer that Google and AI search
tools read is under-optimized and, in places, actively working against you:
**you have no Google Business Profile at all**, your contact info differs
across sites (we found 3 phone numbers, 5 addresses, and 3 emails), your
website's robots file blocks AI search crawlers from seeing your site
entirely, and your main "about the agent" page has no bio text. The fix list
is very doable — most of it is cleanup and consistency, not expensive new
marketing.

## What's working [verified]

- **Zillow**: 29 reviews, 5.0 rating, a genuinely strong bio. Your best public asset.
- **Facebook**: 5,550+ likes — real local audience.
- **Profile coverage**: you exist on every major platform (Zillow, Realtor.com,
  Homes.com, Nextdoor, LinkedIn, LoopNet).
- **Reputation signals**: ICON Agent recognition and top-producer history
  appear across profiles *(exact claims need your confirmation before we use
  them in marketing)*.

## What's hurting [verified unless noted]

1. **No Google Business Profile.** When someone Googles "realtor near me" in
   Lafayette, you are not in the map results — at all. Competitors with
   100–350 reviews are.
2. **Inconsistent identity.** 3 phone numbers, 5 addresses, 3 emails across
   your profiles. Google and AI tools can't tell which is really you, so they
   trust all of it less.
3. **Your website blocks AI crawlers.** A technical misconfiguration on the
   eXp-hosted site blocks ChatGPT, Perplexity, and similar tools from reading
   it. Fixable via an eXp support ticket.
4. **Your agent page has no bio.** The page meant to tell Google who you are
   goes straight from the header to a contact form.
5. **Review concentration.** 29 reviews on Zillow, but ~0 on Google (no
   profile), 2 on Realtor.com, 0 on Homes.com. Top Lafayette competitors have
   79–353. [competitor data verified 2026-07-08]

## 5 highest-impact opportunities

1. **Create and verify your Google Business Profile** — the single biggest
   lever; it puts you in map results you're currently absent from.
2. **Pick one canonical name/phone/address/email and enforce it everywhere**
   (15-minute decision, then cleanup we can execute).
3. **Write a real bio onto your eXp agent page** + fix the crawler-blocking
   robots file via eXp support (we drafted the ticket items).
4. **Launch a steady review system** — 2–4 requests/month, Google first once
   the profile exists, Zillow second (templates are ready).
5. **Publish the 7 local pages we've briefed** (Lafayette realtor, sell-my-house,
   first-time buyers, Youngsville, Broussard, etc.) — matched to what people
   actually search.

## 5 things NOT to waste money on

1. **Backlink packages / "citation blasts"** — spam that risks more entity
   confusion, the exact problem we're fixing.
2. **"AI SEO" gimmicks** (llms.txt hacks, AI-written page farms) — AI
   visibility comes from the same fundamentals as Google visibility.
3. **A paid website rebuild before the basics** — the eXp site has limits, but
   bio, metadata, and content fixes come first and are free.
4. **Buying leads from portals as a *substitute* for this work** — rented
   visibility disappears the day you stop paying.
5. **Review-gating or incentivized-review tools** — against Google policy and
   can get reviews removed or the profile suspended.
