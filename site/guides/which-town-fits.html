FAQ

Common questions about choosing an Acadiana town

What are the actual home prices in each town and how do they compare?

As of mid-2026: Carencro's median is $222,455, Scott's is $247,084 (both Redfin), Maurice's average is $252,357 and Broussard's average is $282,154 (both Zillow), Milton's median listing price is $313,500 (Realtor.com), and Youngsville's median is $314,812 (Redfin). For comparison, Lafayette's median is $240,606 (Redfin).

Which towns have the largest share of households with children?

Of the data available, Youngsville stands out: 48% of households have children, and Southside High School is located there (Jessica Broussard, LPSS). For school performance and crime statistics on any of these towns, review the official sources directly and draw your own conclusions: LPSS, VPSB (Maurice), and NeighborhoodScout.

What school districts serve each town and how are they organized?

Five of the six towns — Youngsville, Broussard, Milton, Scott and Carencro — are served by the Lafayette Parish School System, which operates 44 school sites (LPSS). Zoned elementary/middle pairings include Ernest Gallet Elementary and Youngsville Middle in Youngsville, Martial F. Billeaud Elementary and Broussard Middle in Broussard, Carencro Bob Lilly Elementary and Carencro Middle in Carencro, J.W. James Elementary and Scott Middle in Scott, and Milton Middle School (grades K–8) in Milton (LPSS). Maurice is in Vermilion Parish, not Lafayette Parish, and is served by the Vermilion Parish School Board instead (VPSB); zoning near the parish line can vary by exact address. School zoning can change — confirm your specific address with LPSS or VPSB directly.

Which town is growing the fastest and has the most new construction?

On raw population growth, Youngsville leads by a wide margin — up 300% over the past three decades (KATC). On home-value momentum, Carencro (+6.8% year-over-year) and Scott (+5.5% year-over-year) are the fastest-moving markets on this list (Redfin). Specific new-construction project pipelines for towns other than Youngsville aren't publicly documented — ask Carrie for what's currently breaking ground.

How long is the commute from each town to downtown Lafayette?

Exact drive times weren't available from a sourced traffic dataset for this guide — confirm current commute times with Carrie, since they vary by time of day and which part of Lafayette you're commuting to.

What is the community character like — suburban, small-town, or rural?

Youngsville reads as suburban, with 48% of households having children (Jessica Broussard). Milton is small-town and established, with a notably high share of residents holding a bachelor's degree or higher (47.28%) and many in executive, management or professional occupations (51.7%) (NeighborhoodScout). Broussard combines local dining and shopping with established neighborhoods (Jessica Broussard). Detailed character profiles for Carencro and Scott beyond housing and school data weren't available for this guide — Carrie can speak to both from personal experience.

Where can I find dining, shopping, and entertainment in each town?

Broussard has local Cajun cuisine restaurants and shopping centers (Jessica Broussard), and Youngsville's Sports Complex alone draws roughly 1 million visitors a year (KATC). Specific venue lists for Carencro, Scott, Maurice and Milton weren't sourced for this guide — ask Carrie for current local favorites.

Which town has the most affordable housing for first-time buyers?

Carencro currently has the lowest median at $222,455, followed by Scott at $247,084 (Redfin) — both undercut Lafayette's own median of $240,606 (Redfin), or close to it, while still being on the rise.

Before you decide

Prices, growth rates and school assignments shift over time and this guide reflects figures gathered in mid-2026. Nothing here is financial, tax or legal advice — verify current numbers with a lender or the listing source, and confirm school zoning directly with the Lafayette Parish School System, before you commit to a town.
