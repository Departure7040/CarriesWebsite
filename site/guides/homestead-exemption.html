The Louisiana Homestead Exemption: How Lafayette Homeowners Save

The homestead exemption is one of the simplest, most valuable things a new Lafayette Parish homeowner can file for — and one of the easiest to forget. Here's what it is, who qualifies, what it's worth, and exactly how to file with the Lafayette Parish Assessor.

The Basics

What the homestead exemption actually does

The Louisiana homestead exemption exempts the first $7,500 of a home's assessed value — equivalent to $75,000 of market value — from property taxation, for owner-occupied primary residences (Lafayette Parish Assessor). It's governed statewide by Louisiana Constitution Article VII, Section 20, with parish-specific implementation handled locally (Louisiana Legislature).

Louisiana residential properties are assessed at 10% of fair market value, which is why a $75,000 market-value exemption works out to $7,500 of assessed value (Louisiana Law Help). For example, on a $250,000 home — close to the median in Lafayette Parish — that's an assessed value of $25,000, reduced by $7,500 thanks to the exemption, with actual dollar savings depending on your specific millage rate (Lafayette Parish Assessor estimator).

Qualifying & Filing

Who qualifies, and how to file in Lafayette Parish

Who qualifies

The property must be owner-occupied as your primary residence as of January 1 of the tax year (Louisiana Legislature). Only one homestead exemption is allowed per person or household, even if you own multiple properties — it applies only to your primary residence (Louisiana Legislature).

What to bring

Proof of ownership (your deed or act of sale) and a valid Louisiana driver's license or state ID, taken to the assessor's office (Louisiana Law Help).

The deadline

File by December 31 of the year you acquire the property to get the exemption for that tax year (source). Miss it, and you'll likely wait until the following tax year.

Where to go

Lafayette Parish Assessor's office, 213 West Vermilion Street, Lafayette, LA 70501. Open Mon–Thu 8:00am–4:30pm, Fri 8:00am–11:00am. Call (337) 291-7080 or email info@lafayetteassessor.com with questions (Lafayette Parish Assessor).

It's permanent — mostly. Once filed, the homestead exemption does not require annual reapplication as long as you continue to own and occupy the home (Lafayette Parish Assessor). Some parishes ask for periodic renewal cards or affidavits to confirm continued occupancy — confirm current Lafayette Parish practice with the assessor's office directly.

Note: the homestead exemption applies to parish and state ad valorem taxes but not to municipal (city) taxes, except in specific circumstances such as certain school taxes (Louisiana Legislature).

Extra Savings

Additional exemptions for veterans, seniors & disabled homeowners

Disabled veterans 50–69%

An additional $2,500 of assessed value exemption on top of the standard homestead exemption (Louisiana Dept. of Veterans Affairs).

Disabled veterans 70–99%

An additional $4,500 of assessed value exemption on top of the standard homestead exemption (Louisiana Dept. of Veterans Affairs).

Disabled veterans 100%

Exempt from all ad valorem taxation except for certain municipal tax assessments (Louisiana Dept. of Veterans Affairs).

Seniors & permanently disabled homeowners

Louisiana homeowners age 65+ or permanently disabled may apply for a Special Assessment Level "freeze" that prevents their assessed value from increasing, if they meet income requirements and already have a homestead exemption (Lafayette Parish Assessor). Lafayette Parish sets the qualifying threshold at $100,000 or less in adjusted gross income (Lafayette Parish Assessor).

The freeze isn't a tax-rate freeze. The senior/disability freeze locks your assessed value, not the millage rate — your property tax bill can still rise if taxing bodies raise their millage (Jefferson Parish Assessor). Income thresholds are also subject to change year to year, so confirm the current figure with the Lafayette Parish Assessor before applying (confirm with a professional).

FAQ

Common homestead exemption questions

Do I need to renew my homestead exemption every year, or is it permanent?

It's permanent as long as you continue to own and occupy the home as your primary residence — no annual reapplication is required (Lafayette Parish Assessor). Some parishes may request periodic confirmation of continued occupancy; confirm current Lafayette Parish practice with the assessor's office directly.

How much money can I save annually with the homestead exemption in Lafayette Parish?

It depends on your specific millage rate and property location. The exemption removes $7,500 of assessed value ($75,000 of market value) from taxation — the Lafayette Parish Assessor's online estimator can calculate your exact savings based on your address (Lafayette Parish Assessor estimator).

What is the deadline to file for homestead exemption after I purchase my home?

File by December 31 of the year you acquire the property to receive the exemption for that tax year (source).

Can I claim the homestead exemption on multiple properties I own in Lafayette?

No — only one homestead exemption is allowed per person or household, and it applies only to your primary residence, even if you own other properties (Louisiana Legislature).

What is the difference between the homestead exemption and the senior/disability assessed value freeze?

The homestead exemption exempts a fixed portion of assessed value from taxation and is available to any qualifying owner-occupant. The senior/disability freeze is a separate program for homeowners 65+ or permanently disabled who meet income requirements — it locks your assessed value in place so it can't rise, but requires you to already have a homestead exemption and does not freeze the tax rate itself (Lafayette Parish Assessor).

Are disabled veterans in Louisiana eligible for additional property tax exemptions beyond the standard homestead exemption?

Yes. Veterans with a 50–69% service-connected disability rating get an additional $2,500 of assessed value exemption; 70–99% get an additional $4,500; and veterans rated 100% disabled are exempt from all ad valorem taxation except certain municipal assessments (Louisiana Dept. of Veterans Affairs).

Does the homestead exemption apply to city taxes or only parish taxes in Lafayette?

It applies to parish and state ad valorem taxes but not to municipal (city) taxes, except in specific circumstances such as certain school taxes (Louisiana Legislature).
