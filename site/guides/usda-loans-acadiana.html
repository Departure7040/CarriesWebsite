The Basics

What is a USDA Section 502 Guaranteed Loan?

The USDA Section 502 Guaranteed Loan Program provides 100% financing with no down payment required for eligible rural homebuyers, per USDA Rural Development. Instead of requiring a down payment, USDA provides participating lenders a 90% loan note guarantee, which lowers the lender's risk on a fully financed loan (USDA Rural Development). The loans are structured as 30-year fixed-rate mortgages, and reasonable closing costs and customary purchase expenses can sometimes be rolled into the financing rather than paid out of pocket (USDA Rural Development).

Income & Loan Limits

Income and loan limits that apply locally

USDA eligibility isn't just about the address — household income matters too. Applicants generally can't exceed 115% of the area's median household income to qualify (USDA Rural Development). For context, Lafayette Parish's median household income is $67,451 based on 2020–2024 Census data (U.S. Census Bureau). As of 2026, published USDA income limits allow households of 1–4 members up to $119,850 and households of 5–8+ members up to $158,250 (Neighbors Bank). There's also a loan amount ceiling: the USDA area loan limit for Lafayette Parish is $324,700, effective February 10, 2026 (USDA area loan limit map).

Who Qualifies

Credit, debt and property requirements

Federal guidelines set no specific minimum credit score for a Section 502 Guaranteed Loan, though individual lenders commonly look for 620 or higher (USDA Rural Development). On debt-to-income, the standard ratios for automatic approval are 29% of income toward housing (PITI) and 41% toward total debt; the maximum allowed is 32% PITI and 44% total debt (USDA DTI ratio guidance). These loans are for an owner-occupied primary residence only — they can't be used for investment or income-producing rental property (Neighbors Bank). Eligible property types include detached homes, attached properties, condos, PUDs, modular homes, and manufactured homes that meet USDA standards (USDA Rural Development).

FAQ

Common questions about USDA loans in Acadiana

Can I use a USDA loan to purchase an investment property or rental home?

No. USDA Section 502 loans are for an owner-occupied primary residence only and cannot be used for investment or income-producing rental properties (Neighbors Bank).

What is the minimum credit score requirement for a USDA Section 502 guaranteed loan?

Federal guidelines don't set one specific minimum, though individual lenders commonly require 620 or higher (USDA Rural Development). A lender can confirm exactly where you stand.

How much do I need to save for a down payment with a USDA 502 loan?

Zero — the program provides 100% financing for eligible buyers and properties, and reasonable closing costs may sometimes be included in the loan (USDA Rural Development).

What are the income limits for USDA loans in Lafayette Parish and surrounding areas?

As of 2026, published USDA limits allow up to $119,850 for households of 1–4 members and up to $158,250 for households of 5–8+ members (Neighbors Bank). Lafayette Parish's median household income is $67,451 (U.S. Census Bureau), well under those caps for most household sizes — but a lender should confirm the number that applies to your exact household size.

How is a property's eligibility for USDA financing determined, and where can I check my address?

Eligibility is address-specific and is checked on USDA's official eligibility map (USDA eligibility map), not assumed from a town or ZIP code. Ask Carrie to check a specific listing, or check it yourself before touring.

Can I use a USDA loan to purchase a manufactured or mobile home?

Manufactured homes are an eligible property type as long as they meet USDA standards, alongside detached, attached, condo, PUD and modular homes (USDA Rural Development).

What is the difference between USDA Section 502 guaranteed loans and direct loans?

This guide covers the Section 502 Guaranteed Loan program, which is originated through a USDA-approved private lender. USDA also offers a separate Direct Loan program with its own process; the details of how it differs weren't part of our research for this page, so ask a lender or USDA directly if a direct loan is relevant to your situation (confirm with a professional).

How do census updates affect USDA property eligibility?

USDA periodically reviews rural-area eligibility maps against Census data — a decennial review updated maps based on 2020 Census data (USDA Rural Development notice). That means eligibility for a specific street can shift over time in growing areas like Youngsville and Scott, which is another reason to verify the current map rather than relying on an older list.
