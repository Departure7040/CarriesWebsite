The Home

Composed, Comfortable, and Quietly Refined on Pericles Street

Set behind a landscaped lawn and a brick walkway, this two-story home wears its materials well: brick and cream siding below a gabled, gray-shingled roof, with a side courtyard entry framed by exposed brick, painted trim columns, and a deep blue door. At 2,410 square feet, the four-bedroom, two-and-a-half-bath plan balances generous gathering spaces with rooms that keep their own counsel.

Inside, terra-cotta tile flows through the foyer and dining room beneath a transom window and a bronze chandelier, while a wooden staircase with white spindles rises toward the upper landing. The living room is anchored by a built-in fireplace with a white mantel, its glass sliding doors opening to the covered patio beyond. Throughout, cream walls, chair-rail molding, and warm neutral finishes lend a settled, unhurried atmosphere.

The open-concept kitchen is the working heart of the home, with oak cabinetry, granite countertops, a diagonal tile backsplash, and a center island lit by pendant fixtures. A private home office, an upstairs loft, a dedicated laundry room, and a pergola-shaded patio round out a floor plan built for the rhythms of daily life.

Terra-cotta tile, a white-mantel fireplace, and a pergola strung with light — the quiet luxuries of a home that knows its own character.
