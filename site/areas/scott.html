Buyer Education

What to Know Before Buying an Older Home in Scott

Established homes can be some of the best value in Acadiana — but they come with different homework than new construction. Before you make an offer on an older Scott home, it's worth understanding:

Roof age

Ask for the roof's install date and any warranty documentation — age affects both insurability and near-term repair costs.

Foundation condition

Look for cracking, doors/windows that stick, or uneven floors, and budget for a qualified foundation inspection on older slabs and piers.

Electrical & plumbing updates

Older homes may still have original wiring or galvanized plumbing — ask when these systems were last updated or replaced.

Realistic renovation budget

Price in cosmetic updates and any deferred maintenance up front, not as a surprise after closing.
