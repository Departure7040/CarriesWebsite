Buyer Education

What to Know Before Buying Rural or Acreage Property

Buying land is a different process than buying a standard subdivision lot. Before you make an offer on a rural or acreage property, it's worth understanding:

Septic vs. municipal sewer

Many rural properties run on a septic system rather than city sewer — ask about age, capacity and last inspection.

Well water vs. city water

Confirm whether the property is on a private well or municipal water, and what that means for maintenance and testing.

Flood zone & elevation

Check current flood zone status and, where relevant, an elevation certificate before you finalize financing or insurance.

Deed restrictions & road access

Some rural parcels carry deed restrictions on livestock or outbuildings, and roads may be private rather than parish-maintained — both worth confirming early.
