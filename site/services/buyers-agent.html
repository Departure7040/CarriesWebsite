The Basics

What buyer representation means

A buyer's agent works solely for you, not the seller — bringing pricing insight, negotiation on your behalf, and guidance through inspection and closing. Buyer-agent compensation depends on the listing, the offer terms, and your buyer-agency agreement. Carrie will walk through the options upfront so you understand what, if anything, you may be responsible for before you tour homes. A signed buyer-agency agreement is standard practice in most markets today — Carrie will explain exactly how that works before you start touring homes (sample — Carrie confirms current terms).

FAQ

Frequently asked questions

Does it cost anything to work with a buyer's agent?

Buyer-agent compensation depends on the listing, the offer terms, and your buyer-agency agreement. Carrie will walk through the options upfront so you understand what, if anything, you may be responsible for before you tour homes.

How do I get pre-approved before house hunting?

A lender reviews your income, debt and credit to tell you what you can borrow before you start touring homes. Carrie can refer you to lenders she's worked with if you don't already have one.

Can Carrie help if I'm relocating from out of state?

Yes — Carrie works with buyers relocating to Lafayette and greater Acadiana, coordinating remote showings and timelines around a move.

Does Carrie work with investment property buyers?

Yes — investment and rental property purchases are a listed specialty alongside residential buyer representation.
