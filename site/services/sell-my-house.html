A real CMA vs. an online estimate

Automated online estimates are a starting point, not an answer — they work off public records and broad neighborhood trends, so they can't see your recent updates, your home's condition, or which nearby sales are the best true comparisons. A CMA is different: Carrie personally reviews live MLS comps for your specific street and pulls in what an algorithm can't see. FAQ

Frequently asked questions about selling in Lafayette

How much is my house worth?

An accurate number comes from a personalized Comparative Market Analysis, not a generic online estimate. Submit the form above and Carrie will follow up with a real, comp-based valuation for your specific home.

How fast can I sell my house in Lafayette?

Time on market depends on price point, condition and neighborhood, so there isn't one honest universal number. Carrie can walk through current comparable timelines for your specific area during your valuation call. (Current local timing — sample, Carrie confirms.)

Do I need to make repairs or stage my home before selling?

Not always major repairs — often it's targeted staging and small fixes that make the biggest difference in photos and showings. Staging is part of how Carrie prepares every listing, not an optional upsell.

What fees or commission will I pay?

What you pay depends on the listing, the offer terms, and your agreement with Carrie. She'll walk through exactly what applies to your situation before you sign anything.

Can I sell if I still owe money on my mortgage?

In most cases, yes — your existing mortgage balance is paid off out of the sale proceeds at closing, and you receive whatever equity remains. Carrie can walk through how that works for your specific loan.

Do you buy houses directly, or is this a cash-offer service?

No — Carrie is a traditional full-service listing agent who markets your home to real buyers for the best achievable price, not a cash-offer or iBuyer service.
