Build Instead?

The custom-build path: when nothing on the market is the house

Above $500K, many Acadiana buyers stop shopping and start building — and that changes what you need from an agent. On a custom build, representation means help evaluating lots (drainage, flood zone, utilities, restrictions), understanding builder contracts before you sign them, and having someone at the table whose obligation runs to you rather than to the builder's sales office.

Lot first, plans second

The lot decides drainage, flood insurance, orientation and resale before a single plan is drawn. Carrie helps buyers vet land the way she vets houses.

Builder relationships

Carrie works with Acadiana builders and can help you compare builders, not just floor plans. (Partnership details — Carrie confirms.)

Contract before concrete

Allowances, change orders, and completion timelines are negotiated on paper — the time to get them right is before ground breaks.

Your advocate, not the builder's

The builder's on-site agent works for the builder. Having your own representation typically costs you nothing extra to arrange up front — how it's compensated depends on the builder's terms and your agreement.

Selling High-End

Selling a luxury home: presentation is the strategy

A $700K home doesn't sell like a $200K home with more photos. The buyer pool is smaller, more discerning, and often shopping from out of the area — which makes presentation, not exposure volume, the lever that moves the price. This is where Carrie's approach is genuinely unusual: she stages her own listings herself, with her team, before photos go live. It's not an outsourced add-on billed back to you — it's how her listings, including the multi-million-dollar ones above, go to market.

Pricing at this level is also its own craft: overprice and the home sits and stales publicly; the comparable-sales data is thinner, so judgment matters more. Carrie's UL Lafayette business degree shows up here — analytical pricing, not wishful pricing.

Do I need my own agent if I'm building with a builder?

The builder's on-site representative works for the builder. Your own agent reviews the contract, allowances and change-order terms from your side of the table. How buyer representation is compensated on a new build depends on the builder's terms, the offer, and your agreement — Carrie puts that in writing before you commit.

Can showings of a high-end home be kept discreet?

Is staging really worth it on a home that's already high-end?

Especially there — luxury buyers are buying a finished vision, and empty or personally furnished rooms make them do that work themselves. Carrie stages her own listings with her team before photography, so the home debuts at full strength.
