Financing

The down payment might not be your problem

Here's the part many first-time buyers around Lafayette don't know: several of the parish's more affordable areas sit in or near USDA-eligible territory, where the Section 502 Guaranteed Loan program offers 100% financing — no down payment at all — for eligible buyers and addresses. Eligibility is tied to the exact property address and your household income, not just the town name, so it has to be checked home by home.

That can change the math completely on a sub-$250K purchase: instead of saving for years toward a down payment, the main upfront hurdle becomes closing costs — and some of those can sometimes be financed into the loan.

Inspections

Older homes: what the inspection will really tell you

At this price point you'll tour a lot of homes built decades ago. That's not a bad thing — established neighborhoods have real advantages — but you should walk in knowing what inspectors commonly flag on older Acadiana homes, so a long report doesn't scare you off a good house (or lull you into overlooking a bad one):

Roof age & condition

In Louisiana's storm climate, roof age drives both repair negotiations and your insurance quote. Ask how old it is before you offer.

Electrical & plumbing era

Homes from certain decades may have wiring or plumbing materials that inspectors flag. Some are routine updates; some are negotiation points.

Foundation & drainage

South Louisiana soil moves. Hairline cracks can be cosmetic — the inspection tells you which ones aren't.

HVAC lifespan

An aging system isn't a dealbreaker, but its remaining life belongs in your budget conversation.

The point of an inspection isn't a perfect report

It's leverage and clarity. Findings become repair requests, price negotiations, or your informed decision to walk away. Carrie's approach with first-time buyers is education-first — the same theme you'll see on her Scott page, where older established homes are part of the market's character.

Budget

Your real monthly payment is more than the mortgage

No page should quote you an interest rate — rates change constantly and yours depends on your credit, loan type and lender. But every first-time buyer should budget for the full monthly picture, because the loan's principal and interest is only part of it:

Principal & interest

The loan itself — the number online calculators show you. Get the real figure from a lender pre-approval, not a website.

Property taxes

Usually escrowed into your payment. Louisiana's homestead exemption can meaningfully reduce this on your primary residence — but you have to file for it.

Homeowners & flood insurance

A bigger line item in South Louisiana than in most of the country. Get insurance quotes on a specific house before you're locked in.

Mortgage insurance or guarantee fees

Low-down-payment loans typically carry a monthly fee — your lender will spell out which applies to your loan type.

How much do I need saved before I start looking?

Less than you might think if the home is USDA-eligible — that program requires no down payment for qualified buyers. Either way, plan for closing costs, an inspection, and a cushion for move-in expenses. A lender pre-approval conversation (which costs nothing) gives you the real number.

Should I be scared of a long inspection report on an older home?

No — long reports are normal on older homes. What matters is which findings are routine maintenance and which are structural or safety issues. Carrie helps first-time buyers sort the two and turn real issues into repair requests or price negotiations.
