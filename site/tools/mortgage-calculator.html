FAQ

Common mortgage questions

How much down payment do I really need?

Often less than people think. Conventional loans can go as low as 3% down for qualified buyers, FHA is commonly 3.5%, and VA and USDA loans can be zero-down for eligible buyers — much of rural Acadiana qualifies for USDA (see the USDA zero-down guide). The right answer depends on your finances and the loan program, so run your situation past a lender.

What's PMI?

Private mortgage insurance — a monthly charge lenders typically add on conventional loans when you put down less than 20%. It protects the lender, not you, and it can often be removed later once you have enough equity. Your lender can tell you what PMI would look like for your specific loan; this calculator does not include it.

What's the difference between rate and APR?

The interest rate is what you pay to borrow the money. APR (annual percentage rate) folds in certain lender fees and costs, so it's usually a bit higher and is meant to make loans easier to compare side by side. When comparing offers, look at both — and ask each lender to explain what's included in their APR.

How do I get preapproved?

You share income, asset, and credit information with a lender, who then tells you how much they'd likely lend you. It usually costs nothing and puts real numbers behind your search — in a competitive market, sellers take preapproved buyers more seriously.
