Competitive Deep-Dive

Here's everyone ahead of you — and exactly how we pass them.

We mapped and sampled the sites of every agent who keeps showing up across your search terms — seven competitors, from a 128-page site down to one running an unfinished template. The honest split is this: some of them win on coverage (page count, blog volume, brokerage scale), some win on quality (real local writing, tagged reviews, a builder partnership), and a couple win on neither and still outrank you today because they're on Realtor.com or Zillow and you're thin there. Meanwhile, the work that closes most of these gaps for you already exists — six guides, seven area pages, three price-band pages, a testimonials page, a mortgage calculator — it's built in site/, but none of it counts in a single search result until it's actually live. That distinction runs through everything below. Verified 2026-07-11

Per-Competitor

What each one does well, where they're thin, and our move

Robbie Breaux Team lafayettehomepros.com

What they do well: the biggest footprint we found anywhere — 155 mapped URLs, ~128 substantive pages. Flagship markets (Broussard, Youngsville, Sugar Mill Pond) get a genuine 8-part hand-written page: live stats, listings, a real market paragraph, named amenities, named schools, local highlights, CTA. The blog is real research too — one sampled post ran 1,400+ words with citations to independent sources. Verified

The weakness we exploit: the depth is sharply tiered. His Scott page — his #1 most-recurring market — is a single boilerplate sentence over an IDX grid, with none of the section headers his flagship pages use. Price-band pages (~20 sampled, structurally hundreds possible) have zero unique copy anywhere. He has no on-site reviews page at all — reviews live off-site on BirdEye/Facebook only — and zero JSON-LD. None of his 23 blog posts touch Louisiana-specific statutory content. Verified

Gap-closure move: build genuinely good Scott, Carencro, and Maurice pages — the bar there is confirmed low for him specifically, not just thin in general. Ship the testimonials page headlining the 185 Google reviews he has no answer for. Keep extending the 6 LA-law guides, the one lane he hasn't touched at all.

James Keaty / Keaty Real Estate keatyrealestate.com

What they do well: the deepest tool arsenal in the field — ~25 resource/tool pages including a seller net sheet, an investment calculator, a flood map, and a school-zone tool. 21 area/community pages (~14 Acadiana-relevant). The sampled Scott guide is genuinely well-written — ~450 words of real local specificity, not a shell. Near-daily YouTube cadence. Verified

The weakness we exploit: the site splits its identity between Acadiana and Covington/Northshore/New Orleans, diluting local focus. Guide copy reads as brochure-generic — no market stats, no buyer-decision help. The "600+ Google reviews" figure is brokerage-level and self-reported Claimed — no individual-agent Google count is confirmed anywhere, unlike your verified 185. Verified

Gap-closure move: the Scott bar here is real, not low — beat it on specificity, not length. Build the one field-unique, static-compatible tool worth matching, a seller net sheet estimator, and keep leaning on a tight single-metro focus plus a confirmed individual Google number they don't have.

Jessica Broussard therealjessicabroussard.com

What they do well: the most complete structural mirror of our own plan — 9 neighborhood pages, 2 new-construction "developments" pages (a builder-partnership showcase, the direct analog of our AHB page), 60+ blog posts, a home-valuation page, a /testimonials page, and live data widgets (census, Walk Score, Yelp, schools) on every neighborhood page. Verified

The weakness we exploit: the copy is voluminous but unmistakably AI/template-generic at scale ("These activities showcase the community spirit…"), and the contact page metadata literally says "top real estate expert in Los Angeles." Reviews are thin everywhere — Zillow 6, FastExpert 6, BirdEye 4. Her 3 LA-law-adjacent posts (contingencies, title insurance, closing costs) are the only real encroachment on our guide moat found anywhere in the field, but scattered and generic against our consolidated pages. Verified

Gap-closure move: extend louisiana-closing-process.html with title-insurance and contingencies sections to defend the moat, rather than build new pages. Headline 185 Google reviews against her 6 Zillow reviews anywhere comparison-shopping happens.

Sean Hettich topagent337.com

What they do well: the best-in-class reviews format found anywhere in this project — /about/reviews tags every testimonial with sale date, city, and transaction type, plus a /about/results year-by-year top-producer page. 353 Zillow reviews, marketed hard in the homepage title. The Youngsville page is hand-written and genuinely local, down to named subdivisions like Autumn Run. Verified

The weakness we exploit: only 3 area pages total — Lafayette, Broussard, Youngsville — nothing for Carencro, Scott, or Maurice. No buyer tools beyond a lead form. The blog drifts off-audience with agent-facing posts on a consumer site. Verified

Gap-closure move: ship the testimonials page with the same sale-date/city/transaction-type tagging he pioneered, but headline the one number he can't claim — 185 Google reviews, where his are Zillow. Cover Carencro, Scott, and Maurice, where he has nothing at all.

Honest Expectations

What this plan can and can't promise

No specific ranking position or timeline can be guaranteed — SERP position depends on factors outside anyone's direct control, including what these seven competitors do next. Their pages age, their content gets refreshed, and new competitors can show up in a sweep like this one just as easily as they did this time. What's realistic is a direction of travel: close the launch gap first since it's free and already built, then work the two lanes that need ongoing effort — reviews and the portal profiles — where the numbers move only as fast as real clients leave real reviews. This is the kind of work that plausibly closes the visibility gap over months, not a promise of a specific rank by a specific date.
