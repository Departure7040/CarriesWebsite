Operations & Lifecycle

A lead that waits 30 minutes is somebody else's client.

Everything else in this audit — the Google Business Profile fix, the new carriebilleaud.com, the local pages, the review system — is about getting the right person to raise their hand. This page is about what happens the moment they do. The site can do a great job bringing leads in and still lose all of them if the handoff after the click isn't built to catch them. This is the machine behind the marketing: capture, response, nurture, and the tools that already sit inside your eXp toolkit to run it.

The single biggest leak in any lead funnel: response speed

The MIT/InsideSales Lead Response Management Study found that the odds of contacting a web lead drop roughly 100x between a 5-minute and a 30-minute response — and the odds of qualifying that lead drop about 21x over the same window. Verified Online real estate leads are comparing agents in the moment; the first credible responder usually wins the conversation. That's the entire mechanical basis of what Zillow sells as a "connection service" — matching it with your own leads is how "leads like Zillow, without the referral fees" stops being a slogan and starts being a system.

The Lifecycle Map

Eight stages, from first click to referral

Every lead moves through the same eight stages whether anyone is managing them or not. Here's where solo agents typically lose people at each stage, and what the fix looks like at your scale — not a bigger team, just the tools you already have configured correctly.

1. Capture

Where it leaks: Leads scatter across the site form, Facebook DMs, an Instagram "DM HOME" keyword, GBP calls, Calendly bookings, sign calls, and texts from past clients — with no single list of everyone who inquired this week.

The fix at your scale: Every source routes into one CRM automatically. Nothing lives only in an inbox or a DM thread. Best practice

2. First response

Where it leaks: Responding when free — after the showing, after the closing, that evening — instead of immediately. This is the single largest leak in the funnel; see the stat above.

The fix at your scale: Contact within 5 minutes, every time — an instant auto-text plus a human follow-up, even when you're in a listing appointment. Best practice

3. Nurture

Where it leaks: One or two follow-ups, then silence — even though most online leads transact 3–12+ months out. The lead buys eight months later, with whichever agent stayed visible.

The fix at your scale: Automated long-term drip plus scheduled human touches, run off CRM tasks instead of memory. Best practice

4. Appointment

Where it leaks: Phone/text tag to find a time — three rounds of "what about Thursday?" cools a warm lead fast.

The fix at your scale: Self-service booking with automated day-before and hour-before reminders, put in front of the lead at every touchpoint.

5. Active client

Where it leaks: Rarely here directly — this is where you're already strongest. But every hour spent showing houses is an hour a new lead sits unanswered.

The fix at your scale: Watched, not changed yet — this is exactly the trap the growth triggers below are built to catch.

6. Under contract

Where it leaks: Not the current deal — the next ones. Chasing inspections, appraisal, lender docs, and deadlines personally can run 15–20+ hours per transaction, all of it time that isn't going to new leads.

The fix at your scale: A system of record that runs the checklist so contract-to-close admin stops competing with lead follow-up for your time.

7. Close

Where it leaks: Nothing dies here — but the highest-leverage 5 minutes of the whole relationship, the review ask at the closing table, gets skipped when there's no checklist forcing it.

The fix at your scale: The review ask becomes a scripted, same-day step on every closing — already covered by the review system elsewhere in this audit.

8. Reviews & referrals

Where it leaks: The referral that goes to another agent because "my realtor" faded from memory. Past clients and sphere are the cheapest lead source in real estate — and the one no portal can charge you for.

The fix at your scale: A systematic past-client program — home-anniversary touches, annual check-ins, consistent social presence — especially valuable for luxury and builder-partnership business, which runs on reputation more than search traffic. Best practice

Not worth buying yet

  • A standalone CRM (Follow Up Boss, Sierra, etc.) — real monthly cost duplicating what's already included through eXp. Revisit only if BoldTrail is genuinely fought-with after an honest configuration effort. Best practice
  • Paid portal leads (Zillow Premier, etc.) — this is the exact spend this whole project exists to make optional.
  • An AI chatbot or ISA subscription — solves a volume problem you don't have yet.
  • Dialers and mass-texting platforms — team-scale tools for a stage you're not at.
