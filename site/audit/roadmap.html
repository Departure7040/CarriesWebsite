The Plan

The 30 / 60 / 90 day plan.

Every fix in this audit lands somewhere on this timeline. The order isn't arbitrary: days 1–30 are almost entirely about protecting and unlocking what already exists — the Google Business Profile chief among it — before any new content gets built on top of it. Nothing here happens without Carrie's sign-off at the decision points noted below.

Days 1–30 · Foundation

Fix what's broken. Mostly access and identity — not yet visible to leads.

  • Get manager access to the existing Google Business Profile (185 reviews, 5.0 — already live). Carrie + Brook
  • Fix the GBP's keyword/area-decorated name — one careful, documented change, Carrie's sign-off first. Carrie confirms → Brook edits
  • Replace the GBP website link's fbclid tracking artifact with a clean UTM link.
  • Decide the canonical name / phone / address / email — a short decision from Carrie; the GBP's own phone and address corroborate one existing variant each. Carrie
  • Get access to GA4, Search Console, and the eXp site editor.
  • Correct the outlier phone numbers (Homes.com, LoopNet) and align the 5 address variants once the canonical one is set.
  • Write and publish the real bio on the eXp agent page; file the eXp/BoldTrail support ticket for the robots.txt and canonical-tag issues.
  • Once manager access is granted: GBP categories, services, description, hours, first photo batch, seeded Q&A.
  • Launch the review-request system — 2–4 asks/month, split between Google and the platforms that need it more (Realtor.com, Homes.com).

Days 31–60 · Build

Content and consistency. Early visibility signals start to show.

  • Publish the first 3 core pages: Lafayette Realtor hub, Sell My House Lafayette, First-Time Home Buyer Lafayette.
  • Strengthen the remaining profiles — LinkedIn headline/summary, Homes.com and Realtor.com bios matched to the canonical entity, Instagram bio link.
  • Add internal links and CTAs across the new content; sticky mobile call button if the platform allows it.
  • Start the GBP posting cadence — weekly, from the prepared posts.
  • Begin closing the competitor gaps that matter most: area-page coverage, multi-platform reviews.
  • Review-pace check: on track for roughly 5–10 new Google reviews.

Days 61–90 · Grow

Expand and measure. First real read on what's working.

  • Publish the remaining briefed pages: Youngsville, Broussard, Listing Agent, Buyer's Agent.
  • Mine Search Console queries from the first 60 days for the next round of content targets.
  • Improve conversion paths using GA4 data — form placement, call-click behavior.
  • Build legitimate local authority: Realtor Association of Acadiana profile link, real local sponsorships/events, local press where it's genuine. No bought links.
  • Evaluate: ranking movement, GBP actions (calls, direction requests), leads by source. Brook reports, Carrie decides next steps

Honest Expectations

What "working" looks like

30 days

GBP name-policy risk fixed and manager access secured; identity consistent; site crawlable; bio published. Mostly infrastructure — leads may not move yet.

60 days

Appearing in the map pack for some branded and near-me searches; first content pages indexed; review count climbing.

90 days

Measurable organic/GBP lead events in GA4; ranking movement on city + service queries. Local SEO compounds — months 4–6 typically outperform months 1–3 if cadence holds.

No ranking positions are guaranteed, by us or anyone.

Ready to start?

The first step isn't a contract — it's a 15-minute call to get through the short list of decisions only Carrie can make (canonical name/phone/address, GBP access, which claims to confirm). Nothing on this roadmap moves before that conversation.
