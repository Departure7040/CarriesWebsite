Finding 4 · Competitor gap analysis

Who's winning your searches — and how.

We looked at the agents who show up repeatedly across 11 Lafayette-area search queries — "Lafayette LA realtor," "best realtor Lafayette LA," Youngsville- and Broussard-specific searches, and more — and compared what they have that Carrie doesn't yet. The honest headline: Carrie's review strength is real, but it's concentrated on Google while the competitors below are strongest on other platforms entirely. That comparison needs a caveat before any chart, so here it is.

Pattern

What winners have in common

Review volume & recency

100+ reviews, consolidated across platforms (Zillow, Realtor.com, BirdEye, Experience.com, FastExpert) rather than concentrated on one. Verified

Dedicated local landing pages

Standalone domains with area-specific pages (Jessica Broussard's site, Robbie Breaux's /youngsville and /broussard pages, Keaty's team site) consistently outrank agents without them for local + geographic search terms. Inferred

Multi-channel social & content

Top performers run 3+ active social channels with video and educational content — buyer/seller guides, market updates — not just a single platform.

What winners do on-page

A deeper look at the top-ranking pages themselves (not just the profiles) found a few repeatable structures worth naming: Sean Hettich runs a dedicated reviews page where each testimonial is tagged with the sale date, city, and transaction type — not just a star badge. Jessica Broussard splits her homepage CTA three ways (Buy / Sell / Let's Connect) instead of one generic "contact" button. And Robbie Breaux's area pages follow the same skeleton every time — market snapshot, subdivisions, amenities, schools, agent CTA — a structure that independently ranked for a named micro-community search. None of this is copied into any of Carrie's content; it's cited here only to show the pattern worth adapting. Verified

Fixing This

Closing the gap

No specific ranking position or timeline can be promised — SERP position depends on factors outside anyone's direct control, including competitor activity and algorithm changes. What's realistic is a direction of travel: protect and extend the Google review lead while building Realtor.com and Homes.com toward a more competitive baseline; stand up a dedicated site with a small number of genuinely local, agent-written pages; and expand social content to match the multi-channel pattern above. Sustained over several months, this is the kind of work that plausibly closes the visibility gap — it is not a guarantee of a specific rank by a specific date.
