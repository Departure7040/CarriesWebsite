Finding 5 · AI search visibility

Can ChatGPT and Google AI find you? Right now, no.

AI search visibility isn't a separate discipline with its own tricks — it's the downstream result of three things done well: entity clarity (every surface agrees on who Carrie is), authority (independent, verifiable signals she's real and good at her job), and useful, crawlable content (pages a machine can actually read and quote). Google's AI Overviews pull from the same crawled, indexed pages as classic Search. ChatGPT-style assistants run their own live fetches and cross-check whatever they can reach. Both converge on the same requirement, and right now Carrie's site fails the first gate: it's not crawlable by most of them at all.

The Gating Issue

Nothing else here matters until this is fixed

robots.txt blocks the entire site from most AI crawlers

What: The site's robots.txt contains a duplicate "all crawlers" rule, and the second, more restrictive one wins under how search engines merge same-named rules. Only Googlebot, Bingbot, and a handful of other explicitly named bots get through — GPTBot, PerplexityBot, ClaudeBot, Google-Extended and others are disallowed from the entire site. Verified

Why it matters: No amount of good bio content or schema markup matters if the bots doing the reading are blocked from fetching the site at all. This is the single highest-priority technical fix in the whole engagement.

Who can fix it: Template-level, controlled by the BoldTrail platform. eXp support ticket

Fixing This

The 5 questions an AI must answer before it will cite her

1. WHO is she?

Current state: Her name appears as "Carrie Billeaud," "Carrie Billeaud Team," "Carrie Billeaud-Realtor EXP Realty," and now also the decorated GBP variant, depending on platform. Verified

Fix: Standardize on "Carrie Billeaud" as the individual entity name everywhere, and write one canonical bio (300–500 words) that gets republished, consistently, on every profile — starting with the currently-empty eXp "About Carrie" section.

2. WHERE does she serve?

Current state: The service-area list is already consistent across sources — this is a formatting gap, not a data gap. Verified

Fix: State it as one plain sentence near the top of the bio ("Carrie serves buyers and sellers across Lafayette and the greater Acadiana region, including Youngsville, Broussard, Carencro...") — plain sentences get quoted by generative systems; bare sidebar lists don't.

3. WHAT does she specialize in?

Current state: Specialty lists vary in length and content across platforms — 4 on Realtor.com, 5 on Homes.com/eXp, 6 on Zillow, 10 on Zillow Team. Verified

Fix: Collapse into one true, canonical short list and use it everywhere. A shorter, consistent list reads as more authoritative to an AI system than a longer, inconsistent one. Verify with Carrie

Hold every unverified production figure out of new copy and schema until Carrie confirms it — publishing a number an AI system can't corroborate elsewhere recreates the exact consistency problem this plan is trying to fix. Realistic Expectations

This is months, not days

No one — including this audit — can guarantee inclusion in any specific AI Overview, ChatGPT answer, or Knowledge Panel. Google's crawl-to-reindex cycle for a site this size typically takes weeks after a fix like the robots.txt correction; schema additions take further time to be parsed and trusted; and third-party platforms each run their own re-indexing schedule. AI answer engines built on top of these systems inherit those delays, plus their own crawl cycles, some of which aren't publicly documented and can't be sped up. The honest framing: these fixes remove the barriers currently preventing visibility and put real, verifiable, differentiated content in front of the systems that decide who gets cited. They don't purchase a guaranteed outcome — and anyone promising a specific citation or ranking on a specific timeline isn't being straight with you.
