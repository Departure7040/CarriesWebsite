Flipping the Lens

Search visibility is one game. Sales volume is the one you're playing for.

Every other page in this audit is about ranking — showing up in the searches Lafayette buyers and sellers actually type. But ranking is a proxy, not the goal. If some agent in this market had outsold everyone else 2:1 with zero web presence, that single fact would prove SEO is optional for winning here, and this entire project would need a rewrite. So before recommending more search work, it's worth checking: does the SEO leaderboard even resemble the sales leaderboard? Here's the second scoreboard — who's actually closing the most houses, how much it overlaps with who shows up in search, and where you sit on both.

The Overlap

Three groups, three lessons

Both lists

Tassie Fonseca, the Robbie Breaux Team, and the Keaty brokerage brand show up on both leaderboards — roughly 6 to 7 of the SEO top 10 carry real production signal at some scale. But the mechanism differs: Fonseca produces so much that portals rank for her; Keaty ranks as a brand, not through its individual producers. Inferred

Sell big, invisible in search

Matthew Delcambre, Nah Senpeng, and the entire Real Producers Acadiana top 4 carry some of the strongest production signals in the dataset and have zero presence in the SEO top 10. They're winning on sphere, referral, brokerage lead-routing, and team ops — not search. Inferred

Visible but production-light

Kris Bourque holds SEO position #10 on 22 lifetime sales and $708K in the last 12 months [source-shown]; Cassidy Stoma recurs across 7 market tags on fragmented, low-confidence numbers. Cautionary case: ranking alone converts nothing. A SERP position is a lit shop window, not a sale.

That combination — real but under-displayed production, paired with an under-leveraged Google presence — is the actual starting position for everything else in this audit.

What It Means

SEO is the asymmetric lane — and only half the job

The market's biggest producers — Delcambre, Senpeng, the entire Real Producers Acadiana top 4 — aren't defending search at all. That means the SEO plan the rest of this audit lays out isn't fighting the best-resourced players in the market; it's claiming ground they've left empty. The bar to enter the current SEO leaderboard is set by Kris Bourque's site on 22 lifetime sales, not by Delcambre's $41M. A mid-tier producer with 185 Google reviews going after under-defended search positions is a legitimate asymmetry.

But the flip side is just as real: the production-without-SEO group is winning on what happens after the click — referral systems, speed-to-lead, team lead-routing — and the SEO-without-production cases (Bourque, Stoma) show what ranking looks like with nothing behind it: a lit shop window, a quiet store. Search gets you found; it doesn't close anything by itself.

The operational half — lead capture, response-time discipline, portal-attribution repair, review-platform diversification — is covered in the operations & conversion audit →, and sequenced alongside the search work in the gap-closure plan →.
