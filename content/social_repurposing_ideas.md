# Social Repurposing Ideas

**Purpose:** Turn each of the 7 core page briefs (`content/page_briefs/`) into multi-platform social content instead of treating web copy and social content as separate work. Carrie already has an active presence to build on: Instagram (`@carriebilleaud_realtor`), Facebook (~5,550 likes, active page), LinkedIn, and social links referenced from her Nextdoor and Homes.com profiles including TikTok and YouTube. This doc assumes those channels exist and gives concrete, source-page-linked repurposing patterns — not generic "post more" advice.

**Rule carried over from the core strategy:** no unverified stats in captions either. If a page brief tags something `[client-confirm]`, the social version stays tagged too until Carrie confirms it — a caption is just as public as a web page.

---

## 10+ Repurposing Patterns

### 1. FAQ → Instagram Reel ("Quick answer" format)
Every page brief has a 4–6 question FAQ section. Each FAQ answer becomes a 20–30 second Reel: text overlay with the question, Carrie answering to camera. Source: any brief's Section 11.
*Example:* `sell_my_house_lafayette.md` Q3 ("Do I need to make repairs before selling?") → Reel titled "Do I need to fix stuff before I sell my Lafayette house?"

### 2. FAQ → Facebook text post + comment-bait
Same FAQ content, longer-form: post the question as the hook, full answer in the post body, end with "Have a different question? Drop it below." Facebook's older, more text-tolerant audience (and Carrie's existing 5,550-like page) suits this better than Instagram.

### 3. H2 section → Instagram carousel (5–7 slides)
Each page's outline (Section 7) breaks into H2/H3 blocks that map cleanly to carousel slides: one H2 idea per slide, a title graphic slide 1, a CTA slide last ("Ready to sell? Link in bio").
*Example:* `lafayette_listing_agent.md`'s "Carrie's Listing Process" H2 (pricing → staging → marketing → negotiation) → a 4–5 slide "How I sell your home" carousel.

### 4. Staging credential → recurring Reel series
The home-stager credential is one of the few strongly verified differentiators across the whole content set (`lafayette_listing_agent.md`, `sell_my_house_lafayette.md`). Turn it into a recurring series: "Stage It With Carrie" — before/after room staging clips, tied back to the listing-agent and sell-my-house pages. This is a content moat competitors named in `04_competitor_gap_analysis.md` don't clearly have.

### 5. Area page → neighborhood driving/walking video (YouTube Shorts + Reels)
`youngsville_realtor.md` and `broussard_realtor.md` are built around concrete local detail (Sugar Mill Pond, commute corridors, schools). Once those `[client-confirm]` details are filled in, film a short driving/walkthrough video narrating the same points from the brief — "3 things to know about buying in Broussard." Cross-post identically to YouTube Shorts, Instagram Reels, and TikTok.

### 6. Area page → Nextdoor neighborhood post
Nextdoor is hyper-local by design and Carrie already has a presence there. Post a shortened, non-salesy version of the Youngsville/Broussard content directly into the relevant Nextdoor neighborhood groups (schools, commute, established vs. new construction) — Nextdoor audiences respond better to genuinely useful local info than direct pitches, so lead with value, CTA last.

### 7. First-time-buyer guide → multi-part Reel/TikTok series
`first_time_homebuyers_lafayette.md` has enough distinct H2/H3 sections (pre-approval, budget, down payment programs, common mistakes, starter neighborhoods) to become a 5-part "First-Time Buyer Week" series — one topic/day, cross-posted Instagram + TikTok + YouTube Shorts, each ending with "full guide link in bio."

### 8. Common-mistakes section → single high-retention Reel
The "Common First-Time Buyer Mistakes" H2 in the first-time-buyer brief is naturally listicle-shaped ("3 mistakes first-time buyers make in Lafayette") — strong format for Reels/TikTok completion rate. Repurpose the same list as a Facebook carousel too.

### 9. Trust-signal block → "About Carrie" pinned content
Every brief's Section 10 (trust signals) — team of 4 including her father, certified mentor, UL Lafayette degree, 29 reviews/5.0 — becomes a pinned Instagram Highlight ("Meet the Team") and a pinned Facebook post. This is the social equivalent of the `lafayette_realtor.md` hub page and should be refreshed whenever a `[client-confirm]` item (years of experience, ICON Agent status) gets confirmed and can finally be stated as fact.

### 10. Market-snapshot section → recurring "market update" post
`sell_my_house_lafayette.md`'s "Current Lafayette Market Snapshot" H2 explicitly needs quarterly refreshing with real, dated data — pair that refresh with a recurring quarterly Facebook + Instagram "Lafayette Market Update" post/Reel each time the web copy updates, so the web and social versions never drift out of sync or contradict each other.

### 11. Review → Instagram Story + Facebook review-highlight
Whenever a new Zillow review comes in (see `content/review_request_templates.md` for the request process), screenshot it (with permission) into an Instagram Story and a Facebook post. This directly supports the 29-review, 5.0-rating trust signal used across every page brief and is the cheapest, most authentic content Carrie can produce — no scripting required.

### 12. Buyer-representation explainer → LinkedIn post
`lafayette_buyers_agent.md`'s "What Buyer Representation Means" section (especially the post-NAR-settlement commission/agreement explainer, once `[client-confirm]`ed) is the one piece of content genuinely suited to LinkedIn's more professional/informational tone — relocating professionals and out-of-state buyers are a plausible LinkedIn audience for Carrie.

### 13. GBP posts as the connective tissue
Once the Google Business Profile is created (open item — `reports/09_questions_for_client.md` Q1), every FAQ answer and H2 section doubles as a GBP post: same short-form text used in pattern #2, posted weekly, always linking to the matching page. This keeps GBP, blog, and social all pulling from the same source documents instead of three separate content calendars.

---

## Working Pattern

For each of the 7 core pages, treat the brief as a script bank: pull 1 FAQ → Reel/TikTok, 1 H2 block → carousel, 1 trust-signal line → Story/highlight, and (for the two area pages) 1 walkthrough video. That's 3–5 pieces of social content per page brief, which comfortably supports the "2–3x/week Reels" cadence recommended in `reports/04_competitor_gap_analysis.md` without ever inventing content that isn't grounded in the verified/reviewed page copy.
